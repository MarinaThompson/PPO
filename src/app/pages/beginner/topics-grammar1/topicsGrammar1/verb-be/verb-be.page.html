  • Always use a subject pronoun (you, he, etc.) with a verb, e.g. They're teachers
    NOT Are teachers.
  • Always use capital I, e.g. He's Mike and I'm Sally NOT i'm Sally.
  • you = singular and plural.
  • Use he for a man, she for a woman, and it for a thing.
  • Use they for people and things.
  • We use contractions in conversation and in informal writing.
  • In questions, put am, are, is before I, you, he.
