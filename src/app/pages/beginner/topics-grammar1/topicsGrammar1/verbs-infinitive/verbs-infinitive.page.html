Adverbs of manner
I want to find a new job.
You need to practise every day.
When did you learn to play the guitar?
Would you like to be famous?
  • Manu verbs are followed by a verb in the infinitive with to.
  • These include: want, need, learn, promise, decide, plan and hope.
would like to
  • I would like to = I want to (now or in the future).
  • Contractions: 'd = would; wouldn't = would not.
  • Use the infinitive with to after would like.
    I would like to learn. NOT I would like learn.
  • Remember you can also use Would you like...? to offer:
    Woukd you like a drink?
  • would you like is the same for all persons.
would like and like
I'd like to dance = I want to dance.
I like dancing = I enjoy it; I like it in general.
