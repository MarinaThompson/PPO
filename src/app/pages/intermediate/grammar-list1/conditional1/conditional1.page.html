First conditional and future time clauses
first conditional sentences: if + present simple, will / won't + infinitive
1. If you work hard, you will pass your exams.
The boss won't be very pleased if we are lete for the meeting.
2. Come and see us next week if you have time.
3. Alison won't get into university unless she gets good grades.
I won't go unless you go too.
  • We use first conditional sentences to talk about a possible future situation and its consequence.
    1. We use the present tense after if in first conditional sentences.
    2. We can also use an imperative instead of the will clause.
    3. We can use unless instead of if...not in conditional sentences.

future time clauses
As soon as you get your exam results, call me.
We will have dinner when your father gets home.
I won't go to bed until you come home.
I will have a quick lunch before I leave.
  • Use the present tense after when, as soon as, until, before, and after to talk about the future.
