Past perfect
had / hadn't + past participle
[+] When I woke up the garden was all white.
It had snowedduring the night.
I suddlenly realize that I'd left my mobile in the taxi.
[-] We got home just in time - the match hadn't started.
When she got to class, she realized that she hadn't brought her book.
[?] A: I went to Paris last weekend. I really loved it.
B: Had you been there before? A: No, I hadn't.
  • Use the past perfect when you are already talking about the past and want to talk about an earlier past action.
    When I woke up the garden was all white. It had snowed during the nigh. = It snowed before I woke up.
  • The form of the past perfect is the same for all persons.
  • Had is sometimes contracted to 'd.
